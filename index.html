About

I am a final-year undergraduate student at Rutgers University studying mathematics. You can reach me by email: irs51 [at] scarletmail [dot] rutgers [dot] edu.

Within mathematics, I am primarily interested in analysis. Subfields of analysis I take interest in currently include harmonic analysis, analytic number theory, and geometric measure theory. In the summer of 2025, I participated in the Clemson University REU in Number Theory, where I was mentored by Hui Xue and Tianyu Ni on a project relating to modular forms and combinatorial identities. Previously, in the summer of 2024, I participated in the DIMACS REU at Rutgers, where I was mentored by Mariusz Mirek and Leonidas Daskalakis on a project combining harmonic analysis, analytic number theory, and ergodic theory. Before that, I worked on a project through the Aresty Research Assistant Program at Rutgers, where I was mentored by Shadi Tahvildar-Zadeh on a project about the stability of Hamiltonians for the wave equation in one dimension.

You can see my Papers and Teaching pages, as well as my CV (linked below), for more information.
